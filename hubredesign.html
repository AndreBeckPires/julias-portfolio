Arena Hub Redesign

Modernizing a gaming community platform to improve navigation, highlight membership benefits, and boost engagement through a more intuitive and visually appealing design.
Role UX Designer
Platforms Web
Areas Strategy, UX Design, Visual Design, A/B Testing

Background

Arena Hub is a community space for gamers, offering rewards, news, and forums. The original design felt disconnected, cluttered, and outdated. Users struggled to understand the value of joining and found navigation confusing.

Baseline testing revealed major pain points:

  • Page felt cluttered and unorganized.
  • Key sections like “What’s New?” were buried.
  • Marketplace lacked clarity on how it worked.
  • Community posts appeared random and out of place.

Approach

We started by mapping out the core issues and aligning on goals: improve clarity, modernize the interface, and make membership benefits obvious.

Using the Double Diamond process, we:

  • Conducted competitive analysis and synthesized baseline test insights.
  • Explored navigation patterns and modular layouts to reduce clutter.
  • Designed wireframes and high-fidelity prototypes in Figma.
  • Ran moderated A/B tests comparing the old design with the redesign.

Key improvements included:

  • A streamlined navigation and hero banner.
  • Clear CTAs and highlighted membership benefits.
  • Engaging visuals and interactive elements to boost appeal.

Impact

Post-launch A/B testing showed strong results:

  • +20% conversion rate and +20% CTR.
  • Users spent more time on page and scrolled deeper into key sections.
  • Designed wireframes and high-fidelity prototypes in Figma.
  • 9 out of 10 participants preferred the redesign for its clarity and visual appeal (6.4/7 rating).

User feedback

“This is an interesting-looking page, and you can tell love was put into this with the UI’s interactiveness, the blogs, even giving a FAQ at the bottom.”
